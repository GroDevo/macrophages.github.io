Bacteriophages in the Environment

Key Takeaways:

Bacteriophages are very much prominant in the microscoping world. Where there are bacteria, there are bacteriophages.

Bacteria and Bacteriophages
The word "Bacteriophages" in some way means Bacteria consumer. Though, the bacteriophage does not really consume or eat the bacteria, but more accurately destroys it from the inside. The cycles of the Bacteriophage shows how it effectively eliminates bacterium, sometimes even altering a bacteria's fundamental DNA.
Bacteriophages in the Ocean
The ocean contains most organisms, or marine organisms, than land organisms. As such, bacteriophages thrive much more in temperate waters than in dry land. Some research suggests that there are as much as 250 million bacteriophages per millilitre of seawater.
Laboratory Research of Bacteriophages
Bacteriophages were discovered by William Twort in 1915. However, some attribute the credit for the discovery to Felix d' Herelle, who also discovered it independently ay 1917. Bacteriophages were essentially ignored because of antibiotics, though ironically the role of antibiotics has caused a resurgence in research into bacteriophages for the past few years. Mostly due to the impact Bacteriophages have on bacterial populations.

Medical Use of Bacteriophages

Key Takeaways:

The use and research of bacteriophages for medical purposes has increased as a result of the rise of superbug, or drug-resistant, bacteria.

Early Use of Bacteriophages
The use of bacteriophages was surprisingly abundant in the 1920s-1930s since it was a reliable treatment for bacterial infections. Early use of bacteriophages can be traced in the Republic of Georgia, during the time of the Soviet Union. Where it was widely used by the soldiers in the Red Army.
Antibiotics and Bacteriophages
In the western world, antibiotics began to be rapidly developed right after bacteriophages were sufficiently used to treat infections. It was not adopted since antibiotics were much more reliable and easier to distribute. Though, as a result of that, bacteria began to evolve to become immune to different types of drugs. Some can even survive in alcohol. Among one of the feared drug-resistant bacteria is called MDR TB, Multi-Drug Resistant Tuberculosis.
Phage Therapy and Modern Medicine
Approximately 35,000 deaths occur in the United States as a result of drug-resistant bacteria, according to the CDC. As a result, clinical trials and research involving bacteriophages and their effectivity have begun to increase for the past few years. And it has shown promising results. More pharmaceutical companies have yet to invest along with the FDA.
